4.1.2 Fix x-axis

P-value in research are definitely no uniformly distributed, so let’s give this a log10 scale, change the binwidth to 0.1, and dodge the bars.

ggplot(p, aes(x = p_value, fill = p_comp)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 0.1, position = position_dodge2()) +
  scale_x_log10()

4.1.3 Impossible p-values?

OK, what is going on with the p-values over 1? You can use the expand_text() function to expand text to the full sentence of a selection, or add sentences before and/or after it.

imp_p$table |>
  filter(p_value >= 1) |>
  expand_text(psychsci) |>
  select(text, expanded)
text expanded
p < 2.2 Adoptees had significantly fewer years of education (p = 3.3 × 10 -11 compared with group d; p < 2.2 × 10 -16 compared with all nonadopted individuals in the UK Biobank).
p < 2.2 This clock was less accurate than the PedBE clock but still correlated with chronological age, r = .64, 95% CI = [.57, .70], t(308) = 14.77, p < 2.2 × 10 -16 .
p < 2.2 Epigenetic age was calculated using the PedBE clock (L M McEwen et al., 2020; https:// github.com/kobor-lab/Public-Scripts/blob/master/ PedBE.Md), which was highly correlated with chronological age, r = .89, 95% confidence interval (CI) = [.85, .92], t(308) = 24.70, p < 2.2 × 10 -16 .

It looks like these are somewhat non-standard ways of writing scientific notation (papercheck will correctly read, for example, 2.2e-16) that I’ll need to update the module to detect in a later revision. Let’s just exclude them for now.

p <- filter(p, p_value <= 1)
ggplot(p, aes(x = p_value, fill = p_comp)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 0.1, position = position_dodge2()) +
  scale_x_log10(limits = c(.001, 1.2),
                breaks = c(.001, .01, .05, .1, .25, 1),
                labels = c(.001, .01, .05, .1, .25, 1))
